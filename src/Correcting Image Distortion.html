Artifical Image Distortion¶

Images can be artificially radially-distored using Brown's distortion model.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} x_{d} \\ y_{d} \end{bmatrix} = (1 + k_1r^2 + k_2r^4 + k_3r^6) \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \hspace{2cm} (1) $$$$r=\sqrt{(x-x_{c})^2 + (y - y_{c})^2}\hspace{2cm} (2)$$

Here the subscripts $c$ and $d$ refer to centeral and distorted respectively. Whereas $k_i$ is simply a real-constant involved in the model and can be tweaked as desired.

  • Notice that the equation 2 is simple the Eucludian distance in 2-dimensional space.

A more vectorized formulation is $$\vec{X_d} = (1+\vec{K}\cdot\vec{R})\vec{X}\hspace{2cm} (3)$$ where $\vec{X_d} = {\begin{bmatrix} x_{d} & y_{d} \end{bmatrix}}^T$, $\vec{K} = {\begin{bmatrix} k_1 & k_2 & k_3 \end{bmatrix}}^T$, $\vec{R} = {\begin{bmatrix} r^2 & r^4 & r^6 \end{bmatrix}}^T$, and $\vec{X} = {\begin{bmatrix} x & y \end{bmatrix}}^T$.
